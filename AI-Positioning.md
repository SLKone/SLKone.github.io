# SLKone Positioning: A Business Firm That Uses AI

**Status:** Draft for team review. Internal. Source: Management Workshop Sessions 1-3 (June 2026), refined through review.
**Purpose:** The single reference for how SLKone is positioned. Every homepage, Services, and Industries edit should trace back here. The page-by-page execution lives in `AI-Repositioning-Page-Plan.md`.
**Voice:** All copy follows `VOICE-AND-TONE.md`, with two hard rules on top of it. No em dashes (they read as machine-written). Go easy on the word "senior"; convey experience through operating judgment and "the same people start to finish."

**AI copy test:** Every mention of AI must earn its sentence. It needs all three parts: the business outcome, the operating mechanism, and proof or a softened claim. If the copy cannot name the workflow, do not say "AI where it helps." Say the actual work instead, or leave AI out.

## 1. The Position in One Line

SLKone is a management and operations firm that solves hard business problems: growing revenue, cutting cost, and improving how the work runs. The fundamentals of running a business do not change, and that is our ground. AI is the most powerful new tool we apply, leveraged or built where it earns its place, and we say so plainly when it is not the answer. We are a business firm that uses AI, not an AI firm.

## 2. The Market Thesis (why now, why us)

Two forces make this the moment.

**The AI-native firms are the inverse of us.** OpenAI's Deployment Company and the Anthropic and Blackstone backed firm built on Fractional AI are well funded and model-captive: one brings OpenAI models, the other brings Claude. They are AI firms acquiring business capability. SLKone is the opposite. We start from years of cross-industry operating judgment and apply AI pragmatically, neutral on tools and models. We are not selling a model. We pick the right tool for the problem, build one when it helps, or recommend none.

**The talent-and-capacity gap is widening.** Most mid-market companies, and the private equity firms and portfolio companies that back them, run lean. They do not carry the in-house tech talent or the spare operating capacity to run an AI-enabled change on top of the day job. We bring both, embed as the team they do not have, and leave the capability behind. The scarcer their own talent, the more essential we are.

## 3. Pillars (in priority order)

1. **We solve the business problem.** Revenue, cost, operations, organization, M&A. Fundamentals first. AI is how we get there faster, not what we sell.
2. **We implement, embedded.** A small team works alongside yours, the same people from start to finish, and stays through delivery. We do not hand over a deck and leave.
3. **AI is a tool, applied with judgment.** We leverage the right tool, build one when the situation calls for it, and are honest when the answer is not AI. Vendor- and model-neutral, with no lock-in.
4. **You own what we build.** The models, tools, and capability stay with you.
5. **Proof over promises.** Every claim is backed by a named outcome, not an adjective.

## 4. Who We Serve

Firm-level pages (homepage, Services, Industries) speak to all of our clients: corporate, direct, and private-equity-backed. Do not narrow them to PE.

Two buyer mindsets show up on first contact, and a page should serve both:
- The **outcome buyer**: a leader who needs the result and the number. Lead with the business outcome.
- The **operator buyer**: the person who has to make it work, often without in-house tech or spare capacity. Lead with how we embed and what we leave behind.

PE is the highest-leverage channel, since one relationship can introduce many portfolio companies, and the talent gap is most acute there. So the Private Equity page is PE-specific while staying business-first.

## 5. How We Talk About It

1. Lead with the business problem and outcome. AI never opens a sentence or a headline.
2. No em dashes. Use periods, commas, or colons.
3. Go easy on "senior." Convey experience through operating judgment and "the same people start to finish."
4. Be vendor-neutral and honest. Name when AI is not the right answer.
5. Do not echo the AI-native firms. Avoid "deploy," "deployment," "forward-deployed," "AI-native," "build around intelligence," "rewire."
6. No hype. No "world-class," "best-in-class," "cutting-edge," "AI-powered," "transformative," "game-changer."
7. Proof gates the claim. If we cannot point to an outcome, soften the claim rather than invent one.
8. Replace generic AI language with the operating use case. Say "forecast accuracy," "ARR waterfall," "diligence data cleanup," "pricing model," "revenue-cycle reporting," or "SIOP forecast tool" when that is what we mean.

## 6. What Stays, What Changes

| Stays | Changes |
| --- | --- |
| The bridge metaphor and "measurable success" | The bridge spans the decision to the result, and we are the team that crosses it |
| Embedded, hands-on delivery; quantified outcomes | Named explicitly, with the talent-and-capacity gap as the reason we are needed |
| Cross-industry operating judgment; repeat-client trust | Positioned as the thing the AI-native firms lack |
| Jargon-free, evidence-led voice | Plus no em dashes, less "senior," vendor-neutral, and AI never leads |
| "data-driven" language | Kept. We say "the right tools, including AI" rather than "AI-powered" anything |

We are dropping the earlier "AI-enabled delivery firm that uses AI better than anyone" framing. It collides head-on with the AI-native firms and puts us on their turf. Business-first and vendor-neutral is the defensible ground.

## 7. The AI-Touch Rubric

Not every page should sound equally "AI." Grade each by how much AI changes the work.

- **Heavy.** AI materially changes the offering or its speed and cost. Lead with the business outcome, with AI as the engine. (Data and Advanced Analytics, Digital Strategy and Technology, PE diligence and value creation.)
- **Medium.** AI accelerates parts of the work; the core is execution and judgment. AI is a supporting tool. (Operational Excellence, Corporate Finance, Manufacturing.)
- **Light.** Relationship-, people-, or judgment-led work. AI stays in the background and never appears in the headline. (Change Management, Org Structure Design, M&A cultural integration, Exit.)

Rule of thumb: the more the work runs on human trust and judgment, the lighter the AI touch.

## 7A. AI Copy Test

Before any page ships, test every AI reference against these questions:

1. **Outcome:** What business result is the reader trying to improve: revenue, margin, cost, cash, throughput, quality, speed, risk, or valuation?
2. **Mechanism:** What part of the work changes: forecasting, pricing, data cleanup, diligence analysis, workflow automation, reporting, planning, routing, inventory, collections, or model validation?
3. **Proof:** Which case study, existing metric, or defensible experience supports the claim? If none exists, make the claim narrower.
4. **Ownership:** What does the client keep: model, dashboard, workflow, process, playbook, training, or operating cadence?
5. **Honesty:** Where does judgment beat the model? State that plainly on Light pages and in regulated or people-heavy work.

If the sentence only says "AI where it earns its place," it is not done.

## 8. Front Doors

- **Business value assessment.** A focused, paid engagement on one part of the business (or one portfolio company) that identifies where the value is and how we would capture it. This is a business diagnostic, not an "AI discovery." AI may or may not be part of the answer.
- **Proof.** Real outcomes and case studies, mapped to each service and industry. Proof gates the claims (owner: case-study work).

## 9. Lexicon

**Use:** solve, implement, embed, operating judgment, the right tools, leverage, build, the capability stays with you, vendor-neutral, measurable, the same people start to finish.

**Use with proof and specificity:** AI, the right tools, capability, automation, machine learning, advanced analytics, model, dashboard, playbook.

**Drop:** "AI-enabled delivery firm," "uses AI better than anyone," "AI-powered," "AI-native," "deploy" and "deployment," "forward-deployed," "build around intelligence," em dashes, over-reliance on "senior," and generic phrases such as "AI where it helps" or "AI where it earns its place" when no workflow is named.

**The lines that carry the position:**
- "We are a business firm that uses AI, not an AI firm."
- "The fundamentals of running a business do not change. AI is the most powerful new tool we apply where it earns its place."
- "We bring the team you do not have, and we leave the capability behind."
