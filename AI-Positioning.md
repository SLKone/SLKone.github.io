# SLKone Positioning — AI-Enabled Delivery

**Status:** Draft for team review · Internal · Source: Management Workshop Sessions 1-3 (June 16-18, 2026)
**Purpose:** The single reference for how SLKone is positioned as AI-enablement and implementation experts. Every homepage, Services, and Industries edit should trace back here. This is the "what" and "why"; the page-by-page "where and how much" lives in `AI-Repositioning-Page-Plan.md`.

**Voice:** All copy follows `VOICE-AND-TONE.md` (the canonical voice guide, currently on branch `claude/youthful-clarke-k4q5hx`; it should land alongside this work). ## 1. Where This Came From

Across three workshop sessions the team settled one positioning and one pitch. The core conclusion:

> We are an AI-enabled **delivery** firm, not an AI company. The job has not changed: help clients make more money or cut costs, faster. AI is how we do it faster.

The agreed pitch (Session 3, verbatim — the team's canonical language):

> Most of our clients already know what needs to happen. The decision's been made. What's missing is the bridge between that and it actually happening.
>
> That's the gap we close. We embed — small team, deep in the work, aligned on your actual priorities. We get to know how the business really operates, figure out where the leverage is, and then we move. We use AI the way we once used Excel, except it compresses weeks into hours. Your team can't move at that speed without outside help.
>
> We're not an AI company. We're a delivery firm that uses AI better than anyone else. Our clients don't pay for decks. They pay because things actually change.

**Using this on the web (important):** the spoken pitch is a script; web copy is not. On a page, stay in the buyer's problem for the first two or three sentences before "we" appears, and lead with the mechanism, not the superlative. "Uses AI better than anyone else" works out loud from a founder; in print it reads as an empty claim (see §5, §9). On the page, let "weeks into hours" and a named result do that work.

A blunt way to hold the whole thing in your head (a mnemonic, not a metric): **client value = (more revenue or lower cost) ÷ time, and AI shrinks the time.**

## 2. The Bridge: Continuity, Not Reinvention

We are not rebuilding the brand. The existing line "Bridge strategy to measurable success," the "extension of your team" voice, and "where others advise, we implement" already say most of the new pitch. The bridge now spans one specific span: **the decision to the thing actually shipped.**

This continuity is the commercial point. It lets us add AI on top of seven years of repeat clients and case studies instead of trading them away. The relationship and the track record are the part a competitor born in 2023 cannot copy; AI is the part anyone can buy. Lead with the part they can't.

The current voice also already bans "buzzwords and overly technical jargon." That rule is exactly what keeps us from force-fitting AI. We are pointing the existing voice at a sharper target, not loosening it.

## 3. Positioning and Pillars

Four pillars, in priority order.

**1. Decision to done.** We close the gap between what's been decided and what actually happens. We don't sell decks or diagnoses; we ship the change, and clients pay on what changed. Lead with this.

**2. AI as accelerant.** We use AI the way we once used Excel and Power BI: the same analytical job, except the modeling that took a week now takes an afternoon. That line is the most credible thing we can say about AI, because it signals we were doing the work before the tools arrived. AI earns the second or third sentence on a page, never the first.

**3. Embedded, small, senior — and specific.** A small senior team (typically 2-4 people) sits inside your operation: on your tools, in your standups, through delivery, with no rotation to juniors at week six. "Extension of your team" is what everyone claims; the mechanics above are what we can actually show. This is the durable differentiator — name it concretely or it sounds like every other boutique.

**4. Proof over promises.** Every claim is backed by a named outcome or a case study, not an adjective. The pitch is incomplete without it (see §7).

## 4. Who We're Selling To

The positioning has to serve two buyers who want opposite things on first contact. A page that ignores this whiplashes one of them.

| Buyer | Maps to persona | Wants first | Repelled by |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The ROI buyer** | C-suite with P&L authority, under board pressure to show AI returns | A number, an ROI story, a board-ready artifact | Vagueness; "it depends" |
| **The operator buyer** | VP/Director who runs the work; the in-house IT/data lead | Proof you can actually execute here; who shows up; that you won't break what works | AI hype; outsiders "doing our AI" around them |

**How to serve both on one page (no whiplash):** open with the problem (neutral to both), then offer two doors — one path to the ROI ("what this is worth," metrics, Phase Zero), one to the method ("how we actually work," the embedded mechanics, proof). The reader self-selects. The page plan tags every page with a **lead buyer** so this is built, not hoped for.

**The in-house IT/data leader is the silent deal-killer.** On any AI engagement, the person who can quietly veto is the internal owner who hears "outside firm doing our AI" as a threat. Every heavy page needs one line for them: *we build with your data and IT team, you own the system and the capability when we leave.*

**Channel and motion:**
- **PE sponsors are the highest-leverage channel** — one relationship refers many portfolio companies. Two distinct buyers hide inside "PE," and they need different lead lines:
  - **Deal team (pre-close):** diligence on messy target data in days, not weeks.
  - **Operating partner (post-close):** a repeatable value-creation playbook to run across the portfolio — and a one-pager they can forward to a portfolio CEO. That forwardable asset is the channel's actual motion, so it has to exist.
- **Existing clients are the warmest, cheapest pipeline** and the fastest source of the proof we still owe (§7). Re-pitch the AI story to the current book before chasing new logos.
- **Urgency:** roughly a **6-9 month** window before the AI-positioning edge narrows. Ship the lead pages inside it; don't wait for perfect.
- **Front doors:** a paid **"Phase Zero"** discovery sprint (the offer for a warm buyer) and a free **"Five Whys for AI"** readiness check (the offer for a cold one). See the page plan for the funnel order.

## 5. How We Talk About AI

These rules sit on top of the five voice pillars in `VOICE-AND-TONE.md` (Confident not boastful · Clear over clever · Hands-on partner · Evidence-led · Human and humble). They are the AI-specific additions, straight from the sessions.

1. **Lead with the client's problem.** Their situation comes first; SLKone and AI come after.
2. **Show the outcome, not the plumbing.** Show what AI produced — the result, the time saved — not the toolchain. Enough to earn curiosity, not a lecture.
3. **AI is the accelerant, not the opening line.** A page that opens with "AI-powered" is selling the tool instead of the buyer's outcome, and the buyer can tell.
4. **Calibrate by buyer (§4).** ROI framing for the board-pressured buyer; execution depth for the operator. The two-door page pattern is how one page does both.
5. **Don't claim what we can't prove.** No outcome without a reference. Same rule the voice guide already sets against exaggeration.
6. **No hype.** Extends the guide's banned list with the AI versions: no "AI-powered," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "next-gen AI," and no "uses AI better than anyone else" in print.
7. **Differentiate against the real alternatives, not the category.** "We don't just advise on AI, we implement it" is what every firm now says. Name what actually goes wrong instead: most AI help is a pilot that dies in IT or a strategy deck that names the opportunity and leaves; we embed senior people who ship it and hand you the keys. Use the brand's "we don't just X, we Y" contrast for this — but once per page, not as every heading.
8. **A lighter touch is a choice, not a gap.** Where AI doesn't change how we deliver, we say less about it on purpose (see §8).

## 7. Proof Is Part of the Position

The sessions were blunt: positioning is jargon until a case study backs it. Bernardo owns 2-3 named case studies within 30 days. The site cannot get ahead of that proof.

Different buyers need different proof. "One case study" is not enough; match the proof to the belief:

| Buyer | The belief to win | The proof that wins it |
|---|---|---|
| ROI buyer | "This pays back" | A quantified outcome with a dollar figure or a named-ish company |
| Operator buyer | "They can actually do it here" | A how-we-work artifact: what the embedded team did, week by week |
| PE operating partner | "Fast and repeatable across my portfolio" | Speed-per-deal plus more than one portfolio-company result |

Two consequences:
- The testimonial *"keeping the group small, we were able to adapt and move fast"* is perfect for the operator buyer and does nothing for the ROI buyer. Use it where it fits; pair every ROI block with a number instead.
- **Phase Zero needs its own proof.** Asking a buyer to *pay* for discovery requires showing what a Phase Zero deliverable looks like — a redacted sample. A paid front door with no window doesn't open.

Where a number is needed and we don't have one yet, soften the claim rather than invent it. Example to ground "weeks into hours" once it's confirmed by a real engagement: *[insert one real before/after, e.g., "a diligence model that took two weeks now takes two days" — owner: case-study work].*

## 8. The AI-Touch Rubric

Not every page should sound equally "AI." Grade each page by how much AI actually changes the work.

- **Heavy** — AI materially changes the offering or its speed and cost. Lead with the AI-accelerated outcome. *(Data & Advanced Analytics, Digital Strategy & Technology, PE diligence.)*
- **Medium** — AI accelerates parts; the core is still execution and judgment. AI is the accelerant, not the headline. *(Operational Excellence, Corporate Finance, Manufacturing.)*
- **Light** — Relationship-, people-, or judgment-led work. AI stays back-office at most (first-draft docs, sorting a data room) and never appears in the headline. *(Change Management, Org Structure Design, M&A cultural integration, Exit.)*

Rule of thumb: the more a service runs on human trust and judgment, the lighter the AI touch. On people-led work, a heavy AI claim signals you've misread the problem — the buyer's risk there is adoption, not speed.

## 9. AI Lexicon (Delta to VOICE-AND-TONE.md)

The full lexicon lives in `VOICE-AND-TONE.md`. This covers only the AI-specific additions and traps.

**Sanctioned new terms:** AI-enabled · AI-accelerated · embed / embedded · "compress weeks into hours."

**Keep — do not blanket-replace:** "data-driven" / "data-backed" stay on-brand and stay indexed. Upgrade to "AI-accelerated" only where it's literally true and provable.

**Add to the Avoid list** (extends the guide's banned filler): "AI-powered" · "uses AI better than anyone else" (or any AI superlative) · "AI company" (we are not one) · "revolutionary / cutting-edge / next-gen AI" · "AI-native from day one" · "powered by [model]" name-dropping · any AI claim with no number or named client behind it.

**The two lines that carry the position** (un-fakeable; lean on these):
- "We use AI the way we once used Excel — it just compresses weeks into hours."
- "The decision's made. We're the bridge to it actually happening."
